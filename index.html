05 — Engagement shift

Whales are moving from League back to PvP

Pre-change, whales were refugeeing into League (Ladder) matches where their win rates were better, because PvP was unwinnable against experienced opponents. The intended outcome of this change is to make PvP rewarding enough that whales prefer it over League.

07 — Open questions

What to track as more post-change data accumulates

With only 1 day of data, these are the signals to watch over the next 7–14 days before drawing final conclusions.

📊

Win streak reversion rate

How often are whales hitting the ≥4 win streak threshold and reverting to normal matchmaking? If most whales never reach this, the reversion logic is largely inactive and the power band stays on indefinitely — which may inflate WR beyond what's healthy for the ecosystem.

📉

Weaker-opponent churn signal

Non-whales who are matched against Q90 whales and quit — are they churning from PvP? The same-day cooldown should protect them from repeated exposure, but D3 and D7 retention for this sub-segment should be tracked to confirm the protection is working.

💰

Monetisation conversion

Does a better PvP experience convert into spend? LTV and purchase frequency for eligible whales post-change vs. a matched pre-change cohort over a 7-day window. The hypothesis is that winning feels good → more engagement → more spend.

🏆

Rank progression speed

Higher win rates mean faster rank-ups. Are whales now progressing through reputation ranks faster? If so, does that push them out of their current Q90-eligible range and into a rank where they're no longer eligible — making the system partially self-correcting?
